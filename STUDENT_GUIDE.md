# Your Genba Ikigai App — A Guide for Students

*How the app connects to your programme, your workbook, and your growth as a leader.*

---

## Welcome

The Genba Ikigai Companion app is your personal leadership journal for the duration of this programme. It's not a course platform — your videos, readings, and quizzes live on the learning platform. Think of the app as the place where you *apply* what you're learning: capturing real moments from your day, reflecting on the case studies, and tracking how your leadership is evolving over time.

Everything in the app connects back to your workbook. When your facilitator or workbook says "record this in the app," this guide will tell you exactly where to go and what to do.

---

## First Time: Getting Started

When you open the app for the first time, you'll be walked through a short onboarding flow — five screens that take about five minutes.

**Step 1 — Welcome**
A brief introduction connecting the app to the Genba Ikigai programme.

**Step 2 — What's inside**
A quick preview of the three things you'll use most: case studies, your journal, and your progress tracker.

**Step 3 — Your baseline**
Four sliders appear. Rate yourself honestly from 1–10 on each one:

- *Clarity on my leadership purpose right now*
- *My team's ability to solve problems without me*
- *Consistency of my daily self-reflection habit*
- *The level of trust in my team right now*

There are no right or wrong answers. This is simply your starting point — a snapshot of where you are today so you can see how far you've come by the end of the programme.

**Step 4 — Meet the characters**
You'll be introduced to Sarah Chen and Kenji Tanaka — the two people whose leadership journey you'll follow in every case study.

> *Sarah has eight years in production management. She's highly competent, deeply trusted, and quietly exhausted. She has built a team that depends on her for everything — and she's only now beginning to see what that cost.*
>
> *Kenji is an external Genba coach with decades of experience walking production floors. He carries no slides and asks more than he answers.*

**Step 5 — Your modules**
All five learning modules are shown. You start with the Introduction. Each one unlocks after you complete the one before it.

## The Home Screen

Home is your daily starting point. It shows you three things at a glance.

### Your Course Journey (the main card)

This is the most prominent feature on the screen. It shows:

- Which modules you've completed (shown with ✓)
- Which module you're currently in (shown with →)
- Which modules are still locked (shown with 🔒)
- A single **Continue** button that takes you straight back to where you left off

Modules unlock in order: Introduction → Leading Self → Leading Tribe → Problem Solving → Culture Change. You can't skip ahead, and that's intentional — each chapter builds on the last.

### The 16 Habits House

Below the journey card, you'll see the Habits House — a visual diagram of the 16 leadership habits at the heart of the Genba Ikigai model. As you move through the programme, the section of the house that connects to your current module is highlighted in amber. It's a gentle reminder of the habits you're focusing on right now.

### Daily Practice (tap to expand)

A collapsible drawer gives you access to your daily leadership practice:

- **Today's Habit** — A specific leadership habit to focus on during your day
- **Streak** — How many days in a row you've checked in
- **Weekly Intention** — The intention you set for the week
- **Daily Reflection** — A rotating prompt to pause and think
- **Check In** — A quick daily log for your habit practice

## Case Studies

Tap **Case Study** in the navigation bar to open the current module.

Each case study is written as a scene-by-scene conversation between Sarah and Kenji. You read it at your own pace — tap **Continue** to reveal the next scene. The story unfolds one moment at a time, much like watching a conversation happen in real life.

Each module is structured in four acts. A short heading appears between acts so you always know where you are in the story.

**When you reach the end of the chapter:**

A PDCA card appears — four prompts (Plan, Do, Check, Act) to help you think about how you'll apply what you've just read to your own leadership context. You don't type your answers here; use your journal or workbook for that.

Tap **Reflect →** to continue.

### Reflection Questions

Three questions appear — these come directly from your workbook. Answer at least one honestly. There's no word count to hit; even a sentence or two is worthwhile.

Tap **Save** when you're done.

### Completion Checklist

After saving your reflection, a four-item checklist appears to confirm where you are:

1. ✓ Complete onboarding
2. ✓ Read the case study and answer reflection questions
3. ☐ Record a Genba Ikigai Moment — tap **Record now →** to go straight to your journal
4. ☐ Complete the knowledge check quiz — tap **Open quiz ↗** to open it on the learning platform

Your workbook will reference both of the remaining steps. The app makes it easy to do them in one go before moving on.

## Your Journal

Tap **Journal** in the navigation bar. The journal has two tabs.

### Entries

All your recorded entries appear here, sorted with the newest at the top. You can filter by type using the chips at the top of the screen:

- **All** — everything you've saved
- **Moments** — Genba Ikigai Moments
- **Reflections** — Stop and Think entries and case study reflections
- **Habits** — daily check-ins
- **Intentions** — weekly intentions

Tap any entry to read it in full.

### My Journey

This tab shows your baseline snapshots over time. Once you have two or more saved, a **Growth Summary** appears — a simple before-and-after comparison across the four leadership dimensions. This is what your workbook refers to when it asks you to "review your baseline progress."

## Creating a New Entry

From the Journal screen, tap **+ New Entry** in the top right. Three entry types appear.

---

### ⬤ Genba Ikigai Moment

Use this to capture a real leadership moment — something that happened at work this week, big or small.

Your workbook calls this **Journal → New Entry → Genba Ikigai Moment**.

Fill in the four fields:

1. **The situation** — What was happening? Who was there? What was the context?
2. **Who was involved** — The people present in this moment.
3. **Why it felt significant** — What made this moment worth capturing?

Then, optionally, tag which Ikigai circle the moment connects to:

- *What I Love*
- *What I am Good At*
- *What the World Needs*
- *What I am Valued For*

> **Tip:** You don't need to perfectly categorise every moment. If one circle feels right, choose it. If none feel right, leave it blank.

---

### ◈ Reflection — Stop and Think

A structured weekly reflection using prompts from your current module.

Your workbook calls this **Journal → New Entry → Reflection — Stop and Think**.

1. Select the module you're working through.
2. If that module has multiple parts, select the part you're reflecting on.
3. Write into the prompts that feel most alive for you.
4. Add any free-form thoughts at the bottom if you want.

There's no pressure to answer every prompt. Quality over quantity.

---

### ◎ Baseline

A self-assessment using the same four dimensions as your onboarding.

Your workbook calls this **My Journey → Baseline**.

Rate yourself now across:

- *Clarity on my leadership purpose right now*
- *My team's ability to solve problems without me*
- *Consistency of my daily self-reflection habit*
- *The level of trust in my team right now*

Add any notes that explain your scores, then save. Each snapshot is stored alongside all your previous ones so you can track the full arc.

> **Recommendation:** Take a new baseline snapshot at the start of each module — one per module gives you the clearest picture of how you're growing.

## The 16 Habits House

The Habits House sits on the Home screen and is also accessible throughout the app. It's a visual map of the 16 leadership habits organised across four pillars:

- **Foundation** — the habits that underpin everything
- **Tribe** — leading your team and the people around you
- **Problem Solving** — approaching challenges systematically
- **Culture Change** — creating lasting change in your environment

As you progress through the programme, the section of the house connected to your current module is highlighted. This is a gentle way of keeping the relevant habits visible without having to memorise a list.

---

## Daily Check-In

Tap **Check In** from the Daily Practice drawer on your Home screen.

The check-in is a quick daily log — a brief moment to rate how the day went through the lens of the habit you're practising. Consistent check-ins build your streak and fill in your progress charts over time.

If you miss a day, just pick up again the next day. The streak is a tool, not a scorecard.

---

## Progress

Tap **Progress** in the navigation bar to see your check-in history, habit scores over time, and overall programme engagement. This screen is most useful from Week 3 onwards, once you've built up enough data to see patterns.

---

## Compass *(available to some participants)*

If your organisation or facilitator has enabled Compass features, you'll see an additional section with two tools:

**BUS Check-in** — A framework for decoding a difficult team moment by looking at Behaviour, Understanding, and State. Use this when something happened with a team member or colleague that you're still trying to make sense of.

**Ikigai Map** — A personal map where you place your passions, strengths, vocation, and mission. This deepens the ikigai reflection from the Introduction module.

## Tips for Getting the Most from the Programme

**Read the case study before writing your journal entry.** After the final scene, the app takes you directly to reflection questions and then to the journal — pre-loaded with the chapter's prompt. Follow that flow rather than going to the journal separately.

**Capture Genba Moments close to when they happen.** A quick rough note written right after a meaningful moment is more honest and more useful than a polished entry written three days later.

**Use Stop and Think weekly.** Your workbook will prompt you. Keep entries brief — a few sentences per prompt is enough. Consistency matters more than length.

**Take a baseline at the start of each module.** One snapshot per module gives you a clear before-and-after picture by the end of the programme.

**Let the Habits House be a prompt, not a task.** You don't need to study it. Just glancing at the highlighted section before a workday is enough to keep your focus.

**Don't worry about streaks if you miss a day.** The daily check-in is a tool for awareness, not a performance metric. Resume when you can.
