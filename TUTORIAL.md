# Genba Ikigai Companion App — Quick Start Guide

Welcome to your Genba Ikigai Companion — a personal leadership journal designed to support your growth through the Genba Ikigai programme. This guide walks you through every part of the app in a few minutes.

---

## Getting Started — Onboarding

When you first log in, you'll be guided through a short 5-step onboarding flow:

1. **Welcome** — A brief introduction connecting the app to the programme you've just started.
2. **What's inside** — A preview of the three core tools: Case Studies, Journal, and Progress tracking.
3. **Your baseline** — Four sliders (Self-Awareness, Team Leadership, Problem-Solving, Culture Building). Rate yourself honestly from 1–10. There are no right answers — this is your starting point.
4. **Meet the characters** — Introduction to Sarah and Kenji, the two leaders whose journey you'll follow through the case studies.
5. **Your modules** — A view of all five learning modules. You're ready to begin with the Introduction.

> Your baseline scores are saved privately and used later to show how much you've grown.

---

## Home

The Home screen is your daily dashboard.

**Your Course Journey** is the most prominent feature — a card showing your current module, chapter progress, and a direct "Continue" button to jump back in. Locked modules are shown with a 🔒 indicator; they unlock as you complete each chapter in sequence.

Below that, a **Daily Practice** drawer (tap the row to expand it) gives you access to:

- **Today's Habit** — Your daily leadership habit focus
- **Streak & Mood** — Check-in status and current streak count
- **Weekly Intention** — Your intention for the week
- **Daily Reflection** — A space for a brief written reflection
- **Quick Links** — Shortcuts to Check-in, Compass, Progress, and Journal

---

## Case Studies

Navigate to **Case Study** from the bottom navigation bar.

Each module contains a scene-by-scene conversation between Sarah and Kenji — two leaders navigating real workplace situations. Tap **Continue** to reveal the next scene. The conversation unfolds progressively, one moment at a time.

At the end of each chapter:

1. You'll be prompted with **3 reflection questions** — answer at least one before continuing.
2. You're taken to your **Journal** to write a free-form reflection, pre-loaded with the chapter's journal prompt.
3. Once saved, the module is marked **complete** and the next one unlocks.

> Modules must be completed in order: Introduction → Leading Self → Leading Others → Leading Teams → Leading Culture.

## Journal

The Journal has two tabs:

### Entries tab

All your recorded entries appear here, sorted newest first. Use the **filter chips** at the top to browse by type:

| Filter | What it shows |
|---|---|
| All | Everything |
| Moments | Genba Ikigai Moments |
| Reflections | Stop and Think + Case Study entries |
| Habits | Daily habit check-ins |
| Intentions | Weekly intentions |

Tap **+ New Entry** (top right) to open the entry type picker.

### My Journey tab

This tab tracks your leadership baseline over time. Once you have two or more snapshots, a **Growth Summary** shows your progress across all four dimensions with before/after bar comparisons.

---

## Creating a New Entry

Tap **+ New Entry** from the Journal header. You'll see three options:

### ⬤ Genba Ikigai Moment
Capture a real leadership moment from your week. Four fields to fill in:
- **The situation** — what was happening
- **What you noticed** — observations about yourself, your team, the environment
- **What you did or tried** — how you responded
- **What you learned** — the insight or shift that came from it

Optionally tag which **Ikigai zone** the moment connects to (Love & Good At, Good At & Paid, Paid & World Needs, World Needs & Love).

> This entry type is referenced in your student workbook as **Journal → New Entry → Genba Ikigai Moment**.

### ◈ Reflection — Stop and Think
Structured weekly reflection using your workbook prompts.

1. Select the module you're currently working through.
2. Three prompts from that module appear — write into any or all of them.
3. Add any additional free-write thoughts at the bottom.

> Your workbook directs you here as **Journal → New Entry → Reflection — Stop and Think**.

### ◎ Baseline
A self-assessment snapshot using the same four dimensions as your onboarding baseline. Rate yourself now, add optional notes, and save. Each snapshot is stored alongside all your previous ones in **My Journey → Baseline** so you can see the full arc of your growth.

> Your workbook directs you here as **My Journey → Baseline**.

## The 16 Habits Widget

Look for the **🏠 icon** floating in the bottom-right corner of every screen. Tap it to open the interactive Genba Ikigai house diagram.

The house is organised into four pillars and a foundation:

- **Safety** — psychological and physical
- **Trust** — relational foundations
- **Respect** — mutual regard
- **Responsibility** — ownership and accountability
- **Culture Change** — the central pillar connecting all four

Hover over (or tap) any habit to see its full description and key practices. Tap anywhere outside the diagram to close it.

## Progress

The **Progress** screen (accessible from Quick Links or navigation) gives you a visual overview of your check-in streaks, habit scores, and overall programme engagement over time.

---

## Check-in

Daily **Check-in** lets you log your mood and score the leadership habit you're focusing on today. Consistent check-ins build your streak and populate the Progress charts.

---

## Compass *(Premium)*

The Compass tools — BUS Check-in and Ikigai Map — are available to premium-tier users. The BUS framework (Behaviour, Understanding, State) helps you decode difficult team moments. The Ikigai Map lets you map the intersection of your passions, strengths, vocation, and mission.

---

## Tips for Getting the Most Out of the App

- **Do your Case Study before your Journal entry.** The module reflection flows directly into a pre-loaded journal prompt — don't skip it.
- **Use Stop and Think weekly.** Your workbook will cue you each week. Keep entries brief and honest.
- **Capture Genba Moments in the moment.** Even a quick note right after a leadership situation is more valuable than a polished entry written days later.
- **Take a new Baseline every module.** One snapshot per module gives you a clear before/after picture by the end of the programme.
- **Check the 16 Habits widget regularly.** It's there on every screen — a quick hover reminds you what you're working towards.
